# Audit Outreach Skill

This skill covers the outreach strategy for delivering unsolicited SEO/AEO audits to prospects. The key differentiator: **lead with AEO, not SEO.** Every business owner has been pitched SEO services. Almost none have been told how they show up (or don't) in AI search engines. That novelty opens the email.

## The AEO-First Hook

The outreach lead is always the AEO angle:

> "I checked how your business shows up when people ask AI assistants like ChatGPT and Perplexity for recommendations in [city]..."

This works because:
1. **It's novel.** The prospect has never received this pitch before.
2. **It's specific.** You're talking about their business, not generic services.
3. **It creates curiosity.** They didn't know AI search was a thing they should worry about.
4. **It's non-threatening.** You're sharing information, not selling.

**Never lead with traditional SEO.** "I noticed your website could use some SEO improvements" is the most ignored cold email opener in existence.

## Cover Letter Structure

The cover letter is the first thing the prospect reads. It should be 150-250 words, written from the member's perspective (first person), and lead with the single strongest AEO finding. Include 3 subject line options above the email body: an AEO hook, a data hook, and a curiosity hook.

### Opening (2-3 sentences)
- Address the prospect by business name
- Lead with the AEO hook: what you found about their AI search visibility
- Be specific about what you checked

**Good:** "I recently checked how [Business Name] shows up when people ask ChatGPT and Perplexity for [service] recommendations in [city]. I found some things I thought you'd want to know about."

**Bad:** "I hope this email finds you well. I noticed your website has some SEO issues."

### The Finding (3-4 sentences)
- Share 1-2 specific AEO findings (the most attention-grabbing ones)
- Share 1-2 specific SEO findings (the most concrete/data-driven)
- Frame as observations, not criticisms
- Keep it high-level -- the full audit has the details

**Good:** "Right now, when someone asks an AI assistant for a [service] recommendation in [city], your business doesn't come up -- but [Competitor Name] does. Your site also scored 45/100 on Google's mobile speed test, which means about half your mobile visitors leave before your page finishes loading."

**Bad:** "Your website has 47 SEO issues and your AEO score is poor."

### What You Built (2-3 sentences)
- Explain that you ran a complete audit
- Mention it's a 25+ page report with real data
- Mention the AEO assessment specifically
- Reference the attached executive summary or full report

**Good:** "I put together a complete AEO & SEO audit for [Business Name] -- 28 pages of specific findings, including an AI search readiness assessment, technical analysis using Google PageSpeed data, and a prioritized action plan. I've attached the executive summary."

### The Ask (2-3 sentences)
- Soft CTA -- offer to walk through the findings
- Include contact info
- No pricing, no pressure, no hard sell

**Good:** "I'd be happy to walk you through the full findings -- no strings attached. If any of this is useful, you can reach me at [email] or schedule a quick call at [link]."

**Bad:** "Our audit + implementation packages start at $2,500. Reply to get started."

### Closing
- Sign off with member name and business name
- No corporate filler

## Follow-Up Email Sequence (3 Emails)

The Report Builder creates these in `sales/follow-up-emails.md`. Each email has a different angle and references specific findings from the audit.

### Email 1: The AI Search Angle (Send: 3 days after cover letter)

**Subject line patterns:**
- "How ChatGPT sees [Business Name] (the answer might surprise you)"
- "I asked Perplexity about [service] in [city] -- here's what happened"
- "[Business Name] + AI search: a quick update"

**Body structure:**
- Open with a specific AI search finding from the AEO assessment
- Explain what it means in plain language
- Mention one thing they could change quickly
- Soft CTA to discuss

**Length:** 100-150 words. Short, specific, value-first.

**Example:**
```
Subject: How ChatGPT sees [Business Name]

[First Name],

Quick follow-up on the audit I sent over. One thing that stood out:

When I tested how AI search engines handle [service] queries in [city], your
business has zero structured data for AI to work with. That's like having a
store with no sign -- the technology literally can't read what you offer.

The good news: adding FAQ schema and a LocalBusiness markup to your site
(a 2-hour job) would immediately make your business "readable" to AI engines.

Happy to show you exactly what that looks like. No commitment needed.

[Member Name]
[Email]
```

### Email 2: The Data Angle (Send: 7 days after cover letter)

**Subject line patterns:**
- "[Business Name] scored [X]/100 on Google's speed test"
- "Your site loads in [X] seconds -- here's what that costs you"
- "[Competitor] has [X] reviews vs. your [Y] -- the gap matters"

**Body structure:**
- Lead with one hard data point from the technical audit (PageSpeed, SSL, review count)
- Explain the business impact in dollars or customers
- Mention that the full report has the complete picture
- Soft CTA

**Length:** 100-150 words.

### Email 3: The Competitor Angle (Send: 14 days after cover letter)

**Subject line patterns:**
- "How [Business Name] stacks up against [Competitor]"
- "Your competitors are doing this -- you're not (yet)"
- "[Competitor] shows up in AI search. Here's why."

**Body structure:**
- Lead with a specific competitive finding
- Show one area where the competitor is ahead
- Mention one area where the prospect actually leads
- Offer the competitive comparison section of the report
- Soft CTA

**Length:** 100-150 words.

## Tone Guidelines

### Do
- Write like a helpful neighbor, not a salesperson
- Be specific and data-driven
- Show you actually looked at their business
- Frame everything as opportunity
- Keep it concise -- respect their time
- Use simple language a business owner understands

### Don't
- Use corporate filler ("I hope this finds you well," "touching base")
- Be pushy or create false urgency ("limited time offer")
- Criticize their current website ("your site is terrible")
- Make promises about rankings ("we'll get you to #1")
- Mention pricing in any outreach email
- Use marketing jargon without explanation
- Send the same template to every prospect with [BUSINESS NAME] swapped out

### Tone by Niche

Adapt the tone based on the member's target niche:
- **Professional services** (lawyers, accountants): More formal, emphasize credibility
- **Home services** (plumbers, contractors): Direct, practical, focus on calls and jobs
- **Health/wellness** (dentists, chiropractors): Warm, trustworthy, focus on patients
- **Restaurants/hospitality**: Casual, focus on foot traffic and reservations
- **E-commerce**: Data-driven, focus on conversion and revenue
- **Coaches/consultants**: Peer-to-peer, focus on authority and visibility

## Objection Handling

Common responses and how to handle them:

**"I already have an SEO person."**
Response angle: "That's great -- this audit complements their work. The AEO assessment covers AI search readiness, which is a newer discipline most SEO professionals haven't added yet. Happy to share the report with your SEO person too."

**"I'm not interested in SEO."**
Response angle: Don't push. "Totally understand. The report is yours regardless -- some of the findings about how AI assistants describe your business might be interesting even if you're not focused on SEO right now."

**"How much does this cost?"**
Response angle: "The audit is yours -- no charge. If you'd like help implementing any of the recommendations, we can talk about what that looks like, but there's no obligation."

**"Did you actually look at my business?"**
Response angle: This is why specificity matters. Reference 2-3 specific things from the audit that prove you actually analyzed their site.

**No response (most common):**
Follow up with the 3-email sequence. If no response after all 3, add to a quarterly re-audit list. A future audit showing changes (or lack thereof) can re-open the conversation.
